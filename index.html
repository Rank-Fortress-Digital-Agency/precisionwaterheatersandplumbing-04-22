Why you should consider replacing your water heater?

Your water heater has been with you through thick and thin, heating your water for all those years. But now it's time to part ways and considers a replacement. Here are some reasons why you should replace your water heater - even if it's still working!

It's old

Replacing an old water heater is a necessary task that many homeowners face at some point. While the process can be daunting, it's important to understand the benefits of a new water heater and why it might be the best option for your home.

One of the main reasons to replace an old water heater is because they often become less efficient over time. As your water heater gets older, it will require more and more energy to heat the same amount of water. This can lead to higher monthly bills and a greater impact on the environment. A new, more efficient water heater can help reduce those costs and make your home more environmentally friendly.

Another reason to consider replacing your old water heater is safety. An outdated model may not have the same safety features as a newer one, which could put your home and family at risk. Newer water heaters often come with updated safety features, such as pressure relief valves and temperature-control devices, that can help prevent accidents.

It's not very energy-efficient

Unnecessary energy waste is a really big problem. For example, water heaters account for about 10% of household electricity use in the United States. That’s pretty significant! And if you think that it’s not your responsibility to help out with America's energy problems, remember this: Your water heater is probably older than 20 years and needs to be replaced anyway. So why not replace it now? Let’s take a look at some of the reasons why replacing your old water heater could make sense for you.

Your old water heater could be costing you money

Water heaters are one of the most expensive appliances in your home to operate. They typically cost more to operate than your refrigerator, clothes washer, and dryer combined! So if you have an old, inefficient water heater, it’s likely that it’s costing you a lot of money every month.

Replacing your old water heater with a new, energy-efficient model could save you hundreds of dollars every year on your utility bills. In some cases, the savings can be even greater. For example, if you have an electric water heater, switching to a gas model could save you even more money.

You're not getting enough hot water

When you turn on the tap, do you notice that it takes a while before the water feels warm? If so, then your heater might be on its last legs. And with winter fast approaching, now is a good time to start thinking about replacing it. There are many reasons why you want to replace your water heater instead of just repairing or servicing it: convenience (no more waiting for hot water), safety (a new unit will be up-to-date), and money savings (you'll save approximately $500 over ten years). But there's something else that could persuade you to get rid of an old heater - air pollution. Old heaters use natural gas and oil which release harmful chemicals into the atmosphere when burned. Not only is this bad for the environment, but it's also bad for your health. If you have young children or elderly family members living with you, they're especially vulnerable to these toxins.

There's water leaking from it

The water heater is a small but important part of your home. It heats the water that you use in your showers, dishwasher, washing machine, and more.

Water heaters don't last forever, and eventually, they all will need to be replaced. If yours is starting to show signs of wear and tear, then it might be time to start shopping for a new one. A small leak might not seem like a big deal, but it can cause a lot of damage to your home. Water heaters are usually located in the basement or garage, and if they leak, then the water will pool on the floor and potentially damage any belongings that are stored there.
